Problem Insurance pre-authorization and claim submission is document-intensive, rule-specific, and entirely manual. Teams lack a proactive system that identifies rejection risks before submission and assembles evidence-backed claim packages automatically.
PROBLEM STATEMENT Core Concepts ● Document parsing and information extraction ● Insurance policy rule mapping ● Evidence-based justification generation ● Proactive gap analysis and alert systems Proposed Solution — What Your Agent Must Do 1. Ingest the surgeon's recommendation note and identify the procedure, ICD-10 diagnosis code, and CPT/procedure code. 2. Retrieve the specific insurance policy rules for the patient's insurer and the identified procedure: required documents, qualifying diagnosis codes, pre-authorization timelines, and maximum approved amount. 3. Search the patient's EHR to extract supporting evidence: diagnosis history, relevant lab reports, prior conservative treatments attempted (to demonstrate medical necessity), and current medications. 4. Identify gaps proactively: If a required document is missing (e.g., "ultrasound report required but not found in EHR"), flag it to the treating doctor with a specific, actionable request — not a generic alert. 5. Assemble the pre-authorization form by filling each required field with cited evidence from the EHR.
PROBLEM STATEMENT 6. Score the claim's rejection risk (Low / Medium / High) based on completeness of documentation and historical rejection patterns for similar procedures. 7. Send a submission-ready package to the billing team with all evidence cited and the risk score explained. Expected Outcome A complete, evidence-backed insurance pre-authorization package assembled in under 10 minutes, with missing-document alerts and a risk score — reducing rejection rates and manual assembly time.
